## 1. Introduction

- Industrial Anomaly Detection (IAD) identifies defective products from images without anomaly labels during training.
- Existing methods assume abundant normal training images and run on hardware without memory constraints.
- A promising direction is reducing the dependency on large training sets (zero-shot) or enabling efficient inference under GPU/RAM limits.
- Two lab members (kimjuna0403, chlee1027) are actively pursuing IAD from different angles, providing reference paths for onboarding.

---

## 2. Prerequisites

- **IAD task**: given a set of normal training images, detect and localize anomalies in test images.
- **MVTec AD**: standard benchmark with 15 object/texture categories; each category has normal training images and anomaly test images.
- **I-AUROC**: Image-level Area Under ROC Curve — higher is better, measures whether anomalous images are ranked above normal ones.
- **P-AUROC**: Pixel-level AUROC — measures localization quality at pixel level.
